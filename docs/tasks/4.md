# Improve the web crawling
When venues are found in the search results after the web searches are composed and the searches have been done, it seems that a lot of wasteful crawling is being done on pages in the venues' sites that are highly unlikely to yield events results.

For instance, taking Miami Marketta as a recent find (www.miamimarketta.com):
THe following pages seem to get crawled, or at least they show up in the recent report:

https://www.miamimarketta.com/street-food-lineup
https://www.miamimarketta.com/
https://www.miamimarketta.com/win
https://www.miamimarketta.com/cart

But these have nothing to do with music events. A far better choice would have been https://www.miamimarketta.com/ticketed-events

Hw can we make the stage that decides which pages to crawl smarter in relation to the likely subject matter of the pages' contents? The focus is music and events, not 'cart', 'win', 'street-food-lineup', or any other nonsense like that. Music, gigs, concerts - that's what we're after!
