How this site finds gigs 🎸

Behind the event list is a research agent — a pipeline of steps that runs about every hour. It searches the web, reads venue pages, and saves curated gigs to a database. Only two of those steps use a large language model (LLM, the same family of AI as ChatGPT). The rest is ordinary code: crawlers, deduplication rules, and image caching.

💡 What AI does not do here

Duplicate gigs (“Powderfinger at the Tivoli” on two different sites) are merged by deterministic rules, not by asking an LLM. Crawling, poster downloads, and saving to the database are all plain code too — fast, repeatable, and easy to test.
